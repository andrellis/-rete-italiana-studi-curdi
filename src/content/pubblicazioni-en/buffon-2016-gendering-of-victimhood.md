abstract: "This article adopts a gender perspective on war and critiques Western media attention to Yazidi women after ISIS attacks. Using media analysis and fieldwork among Yazidis in Northern Iraq, it shows how hyper‑visibility of women’s injured bodies reproduces orientalist and patriarchal power relations while silencing other experiences of violence and loss."
